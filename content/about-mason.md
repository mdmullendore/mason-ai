# About Mason Mullendore

Mason Mullendore is a software engineer who works across different stacks, with a particular
interest in user interface and design systems. He is working on Neuro Design System, a
component-based design system organized like the nervous system — Neuron, Synapse, Circuit,
Pathway, and Cortex — that runs on both web (React) and React Native from a single set of
design tokens.

Mason's day-to-day work involves TypeScript, React, Python/Java (here and there), and building apps
that have a positive impact. He cares about developer experience: consistent conventions,
good documentation, and tooling that catches mistakes before they ship (linting,
accessibility checks, automated formatting).

Outside of work, Mason enjoys fly fishing, snowboarding and tinkering with side projects — this chatbot,
mason-chat, is one of them: a small FastAPI backend that answers questions about him using
retrieval-augmented generation over his own bio and project history.

He is known for exploring all of what the great state of Montana has to offer. He is married to wife of many years. Together they have a mini aussie who loves adventure.

## Education

Mason holds a Bachelor of Arts in Industrial Arts with a Minor in Recreation Studies from San Francisco State University. Completed through the Design and Industry (DAI) program, the coursework was originally designated as a Bachelor of Arts in Industrial Design. Due to university-wide program restructuring and institutional faculty furloughs, the degree title was formally shifted to Industrial Arts during his enrollment; however, the curriculum retained its robust core focus on industrial design methodologies, product design, and prototyping.

Mason also holds a Master of Science in Information Technology with a concentration in Software Application Development. This advanced program bridges the gap between technical execution and corporate strategy, providing the framework to leverage information technology as a direct driver of business outcomes. The curriculum paired rigorous training in software design patterns, mobile application development, and API engineering with a strategic focus on product development—ensuring that technical architecture is always aligned with user experience, scalability, and broader organizational goals
